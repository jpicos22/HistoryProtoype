The Founding Vision

One evening in 1948, Aylett and Martha Jane Cotton envisioned a school that would become a haven of learning for girls in San Mateo County. Their dream blossomed into a reality that would define education on the Peninsula for generations.

Their mission was clear: a private, non-profit, non-sectarian day school offering education of the highest caliber — a space for excellence in learning, arts, music, and athletics, where intelligent discussion and the liberty to think freely would be celebrated.

Incorporated on Christmas Eve 1952 and formally announced January 1, 1953, the school set a new standard for education where students would become responsible citizens, free from fear or prejudice.

The Mansion & Renovation Story

Once at risk of demolition, the opulent Uplands Mansion was acquired by Crystal Springs in 1956 through the generous donation of the Crocker family — preserving a Gilded Age landmark on more than five acres of Hillsborough hillside.

The 2024–2026 restoration honors the mansion's architectural heritage while equipping it for the next century of learning. Archival blueprints guided every decision, with modern structural systems woven invisibly into the Gilded Age fabric.
