The Mansion & Renovation Story

We actively chose to invest in the Mansion restoration and digital archives because our physical space and shared history form the bedrock of the Gryphon identity. By meticulously conserving the building’s historic artistry, we ensure that the classic charm defining our campus remains a living part of the daily student experience rather than a static monument. At the same time, digitally scanning decades of physical artifacts safeguards our collective memories against the passage of time, making our rich heritage instantly accessible to our global community. This vital link between past and present ensures that future students will continue to walk through the same carved marble arches and gather on the historic back patio where generations of Gryphons have celebrated at Commencement, keeping our entire community deeply rooted in the enduring values that define us.

The 2024–2026 restoration honors the mansion's architectural heritage while equipping it for the next century of learning. Archival blueprints guided every decision, with modern structural systems woven invisibly into the Gilded Age fabric.

Why We Preserve

Safeguarding Our Shared Heritage

Preserving our physical assets and digital history is far more than an exercise in nostalgia; it is a vital investment in our community's core foundation. The physical architecture of the Uplands Mansion anchors our daily campus life to a narrative of enduring brilliance, ensuring that generations of scholars study within spaces crafted with timeless vision.

Simultaneously, conserving our digitized yearbooks and archival collections protects the human memories, milestones, and personal triumphs that form the soul of the Gryphon network. By actively defending our past against the passage of time, we build a bridge that allows future students to draw inspiration from those who paved the way.
